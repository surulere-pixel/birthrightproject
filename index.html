economic dignity
is a human right.

the birthright project is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit that builds social enterprises, programs, and community infrastructure for economic dignity. we operate across africa, latin america, the caribbean, and the diaspora.

100% of social enterprise profits are reinvested into the communities we serve.

impact lanes

three lanes toward
economic dignity.

every venture, program, and enterprise we build operates in one of three lanes. what proves useful becomes shared infrastructure — blueprints, tools, and systems communities can own and operate.

community wellbeing
the ground floor of economic dignity — the health, care, and security people need to earn, build, and belong.
care is the foundation everything else stands on, and its cost is one most households still carry alone.

financial autonomy
the capacity to earn, own, and build on your own terms — and to keep what you create.
talent and enterprise are everywhere; the tools, readiness, and market access to compound them remain scarce and unevenly held.

impact model

how the work circulates.

birthright uses enterprise activity to fund mission work. revenue from social enterprises supports pilots, fellowships, community tools, and the infrastructure needed to keep building.

in addition, gifts of money, technology, space, and time from individuals and organizations help jump-start the enterprises. everything flows through the oxygen fund — the open giving channel that keeps the work moving.

enterprise revenue + gifts→ oxygen fund→ fellowships + tools→ community pilots→ reusable infrastructure→ more local ownership
