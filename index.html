economic dignity
is a human right.

the birthright project builds social enterprises, programs, and shared infrastructure for wellbeing, digital opportunity, and financial autonomy across africa, latin america, the caribbean, and the diaspora.

100% of social enterprise profits are reinvested into the communities we serve.

impact lanes

three lanes toward
economic dignity.

we build in three lanes. what proves useful becomes shared infrastructure — models, tools, and systems communities can own, operate, and scale.

community wellbeing

programs and platforms that help people move, gather, and access everyday support.

digital opportunity

creative, technical, and entrepreneurial pathways for young builders, artists, and emerging operators.

financial autonomy

tools, ventures, and support systems that help people turn skills, ideas, and community into income and ownership.
